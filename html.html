About This River Rock Calculator

This river rock calculator helps homeowners, landscapers, and DIY enthusiasts accurately estimate the amount of river rock needed for landscaping projects. Whether you're creating a dry river bed, designing a garden path, or adding decorative rock features, this tool takes the guesswork out of material estimation.

Our calculator uses standard industry formulas to provide accurate estimates based on your project's dimensions and the size of river rock you plan to use. It calculates both the weight in tons and approximate cost, helping you budget effectively for your landscaping project.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does river rock typically cost?

River rock costs between $100 to $800 per ton, depending on the rock size, type, and your location. Larger rocks generally cost more than smaller ones. Delivery fees may apply for large quantities.

How deep should I lay river rock for landscaping?

For most landscaping applications, a depth of 2-4 inches is recommended. This provides adequate coverage to prevent weed growth while allowing the rocks to settle properly. For pathways, use 3-4 inches, and for decorative purposes, 2 inches may be sufficient.

Should I put anything under river rock?

Yes, it's recommended to use landscape fabric beneath river rock. This barrier helps prevent weeds from growing through while still allowing water drainage. For areas with poor drainage, you might also consider adding a layer of gravel before placing the fabric and river rock.
